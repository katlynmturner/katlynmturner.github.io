The process for creating technology directly shapes who benefits from a technology and how it impacts societal disparities along characteristics of historic discrimination such as gender, race, class, national origin, immigration status, and sexuality. The proposed project creates new knowledge and evaluation methods to understand specific, emerging innovation trends while considering geographic effects. The work also creates methods to evaluate how these innovation trends relate to inclusiveness of who participates in and who benefits from innovation. This project, in collaboration with Prof. Danielle Wood (MIT) and Prof. Sebastian Pfotenhauer (TUM), explores innovation practices and sociotechnical imaginaries of inclusion and innovation in two American metropolitan areas: Greater Boston, and the Detroit Metro. The work considers three distinct innovation sectors: robotics, urban energy, and the space exploration sector. This work has been presented at the International Astronautical Congress (IAC)--in Dubai, Paris, Baku, and Milan.
